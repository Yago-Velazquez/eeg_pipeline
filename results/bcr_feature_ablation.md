## Interpretation

- **Baseline (all features):** AUPRC = 0.5154 ± 0.0818
- **Random baseline:** AUPRC ≈ 0.039 (3.9% bad rate)
1. Decomposition dominates. Removing the 105 PCA/ICA/residual features costs 0.092 AUPRC — 6× more than removing spatial (0.015) and frequency (0.019) combined. This is the real driver, not spatial correlation as SHAP suggested. The SHAP/ablation discrepancy is a legitimate methodological insight: SHAP ranks individual feature contributions within the full model, but when 105 correlated features are removed together, the model can't compensate. Worth a sentence in the methods.
2. Impedance is useless — and slightly harmful. Run B is marginally above baseline (+0.005). This is within noise (std ~0.08) but directionally consistent — impedance adds nothing and may introduce a tiny amount of noise from the visit-3 confound. Your minimum viable feature set can explicitly exclude it.
